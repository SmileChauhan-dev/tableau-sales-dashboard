# Dashboard Analysis Report

## 1. Overview
The dashboard provides a regional performance view across profit, sales contribution, customer segments, and monthly order trends. It enables quick identification of high-performing regions, segment-wise profit distribution, and seasonal patterns in order quantity.

## 2. Profit by Region and Customer Segment
The stacked bar chart highlights how each customer segment contributes to profit within every region.

### Key Insights
- **Central** shows strong profit contribution from Corporate and Home Office, making it a leading region.
- **East** has balanced contributions from Corporate and Home Office but slightly lower profits than Central.
- **South** shows moderate performance; Corporate contributes the most while Home Office is lower.
- **West** relies heavily on Small Business and Corporate, with Corporate generating a large portion of profit.

### Interpretation
Regions with strong **Corporate** segment profits show higher overall profitability. **Central** and **West** exhibit superior diversification across segments.

## 3. Percentage Sales Contribution & Regional Sales
The pie chart displays each region's share in total sales along with absolute sales figures.

### Sales Contribution
- Central: ~31.5% (highest)  
- West: ~24.5%  
- East: ~22.9%  
- South: ~21.1%

### Interpretation
**Central** dominates both sales and profit. **South** has the lowest contribution, suggesting potential for targeted strategies to improve sales.

## 4. Profit vs Sales Scatter Plot
This visualization shows how profit correlates with sales across all records.

### Key Observations
- Most data points fall within moderate sales and low profit.  
- A few high-sales points show negative or near-zero profit.  
- Positive profit outliers appear at mid-range sales levels.

### Interpretation
The variability suggests differing cost structures or discount strategies across products or regions. Efficiency improvements may be needed where sales are high but profit is low.

## 6. Key Business Conclusions
- **Central** region is the top performer across both profit and sales.  
- **Corporate** is the most profitable segment across all regions.  
- High sales do not always correlate with high profit.  
- **May** is the strongest month; **February** and **November** are weaker.  
- **South** and **East** offer opportunities for targeted improvements.
