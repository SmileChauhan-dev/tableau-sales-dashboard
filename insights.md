#  Dashboard Insights

## 1. Profit by Region and Customer Segment
- Central region contributes the highest overall sales share (**31.5%**).
- Corporate customers generate the highest profit across most regions.
- South region shows strong profitability from the Consumer segment.
- Customer segment performance varies significantly across regions, highlighting opportunities for targeted marketing and sales strategies.

## 2. Regional Sales Contribution
- Central region leads total sales contribution, followed by West, East, and South.
- Sales are relatively well distributed across regions, indicating a balanced customer base.
- South region contributes the lowest share of sales, suggesting potential growth opportunities.

## 3. Profit vs Sales Analysis
- A positive relationship exists between **Sales** and **Profit**, indicating that higher sales generally result in higher profit.
- Most data points are clustered at lower sales values, showing that the majority of transactions are small-to-medium-sized orders.
- The clustering is caused by a few very large orders extending the sales range, compressing smaller transactions on the chart (**right-skewed distribution**), not by a Tableau visualization issue.
- Several high-value orders act as outliers and contribute significantly to overall profit.

## 4. Order Quantity by Month
- Order volume remains relatively stable throughout the year.
- **May** records the highest order quantity, indicating a peak demand period.
- **November** shows the lowest order quantity.
- The absence of sharp fluctuations suggests consistent customer demand with limited seasonality.

#  Overall Findings
- Corporate customers are the most profitable customer segment.
- Central region is the strongest contributor to sales performance.
- Business revenue is driven by a large number of small-to-medium transactions and a small number of high-value orders.
- Monthly order demand remains stable, providing predictable sales performance throughout the year.
